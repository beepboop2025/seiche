1 · Executive summary

What it is. Seiche is a single-pane analytics terminal that watches the machinery underneath US dollar markets: bank reserves, the repo market, the Treasury General Account, the Fed's balance sheet, Treasury auctions, and the leveraged hedge-fund positioning that sits on top of all of it. It converts about forty raw official data series into seven computed "engines" and one composite score, the Seiche Index, with a plain-language regime call: CALM, EROSION, STRAIN, or STRESS.

Why it exists. Every notable market stress event of 2025 and 2026 announced itself first in the plumbing, not in prices. The September 15, 2025 tax-date squeeze, the record Standing Repo Facility draws of October 31 ($50B) and December 31, 2025 ($74.6B), and the April 2025 basis-trade unwind were all preceded by measurable moves in repo-rate distribution tails, facility usage, and reserve arithmetic while equity and bond screens looked normal. The data that would have warned you is public and free. It is just scattered across five agencies, published in different formats at different lags, and never synthesized. Seiche is that missing synthesis layer.

Who it is for. Rates and repo desks, macro and relative-value hedge funds, treasury teams, and serious independent macro analysts. The positioning line: the opinionated funding-stress terminal for people who cannot expense a $32,000 Bloomberg seat.

Current reading (2026-07-06). Seiche Index 39 · EROSION

Structurally tight, acutely calm. The overnight reverse-repo buffer is effectively gone ($2B against a $2.55T peak), reserves sit at $2,967B, below the model's estimated scarcity kink of roughly $3,660B, and the forward reserve path flags July 31 and August 31 as crunch-risk dates. At the same time the acute indicators are quiet: rate-distribution tails are normal, SOFR prints 1bp below the interest rate on reserves, and Standing Repo Facility take-up is zero. "Erosion, not strain" is exactly what an honest reading of this market looks like.

What is real data and what is a model output

The inputs above are real official statistics. The engines (kink estimate, forward reserve path, echo similarity, RV size proxy, digestion index, the Seiche Index itself) are computed opinions built from those inputs. Each engine publishes its method string on the dashboard, and each model carries its own confidence discount. The tool never presents a model output as an observation. Known honest lags: CFTC positioning is published Friday for the prior Tuesday (T+3 by construction); OFR repo series marked preliminary are T+1; reserves and the Fed balance sheet are weekly (Wednesday). The dashboard shows the as-of date on every number, and the July 4 holiday weekend explains the 2026-07-02 stamps at the time of writing.

3 · Why now: the market regime this tool was built for

Three research sweeps (money-market plumbing, capital-market themes, competitive landscape) were run before a line of code was written. The verdict, in numbers:

  • Quantitative tightening ended December 1, 2025. The Fed's balance sheet fell from a $8.97T peak to roughly $6.7T, then the Fed pivoted to Reserve Management Purchases of about $40B of T-bills per month. It stopped shrinking because the plumbing told it to.
  • The shock absorber is empty. The overnight reverse-repo facility, $2.55T at its 2022 peak, now holds about $2B. Until 2025, Treasury issuance drained the RRP first and reserves were insulated. Now every Treasury settlement and every tax date hits bank reserves directly, dollar for dollar.
  • Reserves are testing the "ample" floor. Roughly $2.97T, about 9.6% of GDP. The Q4 2025 squeezes (SOFR-IORB spreads at their widest since 2020, record SRF usage) happened at reserve levels only slightly below today's.
  • The leveraged Treasury complex is historically large. Hedge-fund net repo borrowing reached roughly $1.8T by end-2025. Published estimates of the basis trade disagree by half a trillion dollars (Morgan Stanley ~$1.5T vs IMF ~$1T) partly because methods are opaque. The swap-spread trade doubled to about $631B in fifteen months. Regulators (IMF April 2026 GFSR, FSB February 2026, Dallas Fed May 2026) have all flagged it; Fed staff describe their own live tracker as experimental.
  • Every recent break started in the plumbing. Sep 15, 2025: SOFR printed 18bp over EFFR on a tax date. Oct 31, 2025: $50B SRF draw. Dec 31, 2025: $74.6B SRF record with year-end repo at 4.0%. April 2025: tariff shock forced a basis unwind with 50bp intraday swings in the 10-year. In each case, distribution tails, facility usage, or settlement arithmetic moved first.

4 · The seven engines, in detail

4.1 Kink Engine — where does reserve scarcity actually start?

The demand curve for bank reserves is flat when reserves are abundant (the SOFR-IORB spread ignores reserve changes) and turns steeply upward when they get scarce. The point where it bends, the "kink", is the single most important unknown in money markets: the Fed publishes its own estimate (Reserve Demand Elasticity) only as occasional research. Seiche fits it continuously.

spread_bp = a + slope × max(0, kink_ratio − reserves/GDP) + ε
kink_ratio found by grid search over 121 candidates; weekly data 2017-present; spreads winsorized at 1/99%

Current output: kink ≈ $3,661B of reserves; actual reserves $2,967B, so the system is operating about $694B below the estimated bend, with fit R² 0.62. Crucially, the engine also checks itself against the market: the fitted curve predicts a SOFR-IORB spread of −1bp at today's reserve level, and the market prints −2bp. That 0.91 consistency score multiplies the engine's contribution. When model and market disagree, the engine's voice is automatically quieted rather than trusted.

Interpretation: being below a shallow kink means reserve scarcity is real but early; the slope of the fitted scarcity region is still gentle. This matches the Fed's own behavior (it ended QT and began bill purchases precisely because elasticity turned negative).

4.2 Liquidity Weather — the six-week forward reserve forecast

With the RRP buffer at zero, reserves tomorrow are an arithmetic identity: today's reserves, plus Fed balance-sheet drift, minus whatever the Treasury General Account absorbs. The TGA moves in a strongly seasonal monthly rhythm (mid-month settlements, corporate tax dates on the 15th of March/April/June/September/December, month-end flows). Seiche learns that rhythm from about three years of Daily Treasury Statement history, projects it forward 42 business days, and adds honest uncertainty bands built from the 20th/80th percentiles of the model's own backtested cumulative errors.

Current output: the path drifts sideways around $2.9-3.0T with wide bands. Two crunch windows are flagged: July 31 and August 31, 2026, month-end dates where the worst-case band touches roughly $2,831-2,837B while reserves already sit below the kink. A design subtlety: when reserves are already below the kink, "proximity to the kink" stops discriminating (every day would flag), so the engine collapses to flagging only calendar pressure dates. Degenerate signals are muted by design.

4.3 Tail Seismograph — the tails detach before the median moves

The NY Fed publishes the full daily distribution of secured funding rates, not just the median: the 1st, 25th, 75th, and 99th percentiles of SOFR, TGCR, and BGCR. Before any squeeze reaches the headline print, some desk somewhere pays up first, and it shows in the 99th percentile. September 15 and December 31, 2025 both exhibited tail detachment days before the median moved. Seiche computes P99 − P50 for each rate in basis points, z-scores it against a trailing year, blends the three rates (SOFR 0.5, TGCR 0.3, BGCR 0.2), and smooths with a 5-day EWMA.

Current output: tail index z ≈ 0, SOFR tail 8bp over median, SOFR-IORB at −1bp. Quiet. This is the engine that will move first when the next squeeze builds, and its 24% weight is the largest in the composite for exactly that reason.

4.4 Echo Engine — does today rhyme with any pre-crisis run-up?

Seiche maintains a daily state vector of seven plumbing z-scores (SOFR-IORB, EFFR-IORB, BGCR-SOFR, RRP level, 5-day TGA change, 4-week reserve change, SRF usage). It takes the trailing 30-day trajectory of that vector and measures its RMSE similarity against the windows that preceded six labeled historical episodes: the September 2019 repo spike, March 2020, the March 2023 SVB run, and the April, September, and December 2025 events.

Current output: the closest match is the run-up to the September 2025 tax-date squeeze at 0.58 similarity, aligned to one day before that break. Interpretation discipline: resemblance is context, not evidence. The Echo output is displayed alongside the index but deliberately carries zero weight in it. This is a documented editorial decision, changeable in one line of config.

4.5 RV X-Ray — how big is the leveraged Treasury complex, really?

Published basis-trade estimates disagree by $500B because their methods are opaque. Seiche's method is deliberately simple and fully published:

pair proxy = Σ over 6 UST futures contracts of min(leveraged-fund shorts, asset-manager longs) × face value
DV01 = Σ shorts × per-contract dollar-value-of-1bp (constants in config, tunable)

Current output: pair proxy $1,060B, sitting independently on top of the IMF's ~$1T estimate; aggregate DV01 $557M per basis point; position down $167B over 13 weeks (the complex is shrinking, not building); size z-score +0.49 against its own history. The shock table answers the question a risk desk actually asks: a 30bp adverse move marks the complex down $16.7B, and an assumed 10% forced unwind ($106B) would consume about 3% of one day's DVP repo volume ($3,282B/day). The funding market can currently absorb an orderly unwind; the danger scenario is a disorderly one on a crunch date, which is why this engine is read together with Liquidity Weather.

4.6 Auction Digestion — is the market choking on Treasury supply?

Each Treasury auction is scored against the trailing distribution of its own tenor (minimum eight prior auctions): bid-to-cover z-score (weight 0.5, inverted), primary-dealer takedown share z (0.3, dealers as buyers of last resort), indirect-bidder share z (0.2, inverted, foreign demand). Bills are down-weighted 0.35 since coupons carry the duration-absorption signal. An EWMA(20) across auctions gives a cumulative indigestion index.

Current output: digestion index 0.16, mildly positive; the July 6 13-week bill scored 1.15 (weak) while surrounding auctions were fine. Nothing resembling the tailed 2s/5s/7s cluster of March 2026, which is this engine's reference signature.

4.7 Seiche Index — one number, fully decomposed, fail-loud

weights: tails .24 · kink .18 · weather .16 · rvxray .16 · srf .12 · auctions .08 · buffers .06
regimes: <25 CALM · 25-45 EROSION · 45-70 STRAIN · ≥70 STRESS

The composite is a weighted blend of engine sub-scores plus two level gauges: SRF usage (the "confession channel", since paying the ceiling rate is an admission that no cheaper private funding existed) and RRP buffer emptiness. Two properties matter more than the formula:

  • Full decomposition. Every point of the index is attributable to a named component on screen. No black box.
  • Fail-loud. If an upstream feed dies, its component renders as DEAD, its weight is renormalized away, and the published coverage percentage drops. A broken input can never silently make the index look calm. This was exercised for real during the build: when FRED throttled, the dashboard showed the degradation explicitly.

System design

  • Backend: Python 3.14, FastAPI on port 8787. One collector per agency with per-series fault isolation (a dead series degrades to its stale cached copy with its true staleness class, or to an explicit fault entry; it never kills the batch). SQLite cache with cadence-aware TTLs; warm snapshot assembles in ~3 seconds.
  • Frontend: Vite + React + TypeScript + uPlot, dark terminal aesthetic, served by the same process in production. Provenance table renders every input with source, as-of date, fetch time, and a staleness chip (fresh / aging / stale / dead).
  • The editorial layer is quarantined. Every judgment call (composite weights, regime thresholds, crunch cushions, the episode library, contract DV01 constants) lives in one file, backend/seiche/config.py, so the operator can tune the tool's voice without touching engine math.
  • Design lineage: the provenance-native, fail-loud, confidence-native principles come from the Tiktó design system.
Battle scars from live verification (all fixed, all documented)
  • FRED's CDN silently blackholes custom User-Agent strings (the connection hangs with no error) and tarpits browser UAs sent over Python TLS. The collector deliberately sends no UA header. This took three live test cycles to isolate.
  • TGCR and BGCR return 404 on FRED's CSV endpoint; they are sourced from OFR instead.
  • Quarterly GDP publishes with a lag; naive alignment silently truncated the kink fit at GDP's last observation (January), making the engine six months stale. Caught by reading the as-of date on the dashboard during visual verification.
  • OFR volume mnemonics are denominated in raw dollars, not billions; the DVP figure briefly displayed as $3.28 trillion billion per day.
  • CFTC's TFF dataset drops the _all suffix on positioning fields; NY Fed's last/N rates endpoint rejects long windows and was replaced with explicit date-range search.

6 · Honest limitations

  • Daily cadence, not intraday. The free stack's honest granularity is daily prints plus same-day operation results. Seiche will flag the run-up to a squeeze; it will not tick during one.
  • Positioning is T+3 and futures-only. CFTC data cannot see repo-financed cash positions directly; the RV X-Ray is a proxy with a published method, not a census. Its value is trend and shock arithmetic, not the third decimal.
  • The kink is an estimate. R² 0.62 on a regime-spanning sample; the consistency check exists precisely because breakpoint estimates are unstable. Treat the distance-to-kink as a band, not a line.
  • Composite weights are editorial. They encode the view that tails and spreads lead, structure amplifies, and positioning is the payload. A different desk could defensibly weight differently; the config file invites it.
  • No alerting yet (designed for, not built), no swap-spread levels (needs paid data; the funding leg is proxied instead), single-user, local-first.
  • Not investment advice. It is an instrument panel, not a trade recommendation engine.

7 · Roadmap

  1. Alerting — webhook/email/Telegram on regime transitions, tail-z thresholds, SRF take-up > 0, new crunch windows. The payload structure already supports it.
  2. Auction calendar overlay — merge FiscalData's upcoming-auctions feed into Liquidity Weather so crunch windows show the specific settlements that cause them.
  3. Primary dealer positions (NY Fed FR2004) as a dealer balance-sheet constraint gauge, and MMF flow decomposition from the OFR MMF dataset.
  4. Echo library growth — each new stress episode gets labeled and joins the fingerprint library, making the engine smarter with every cycle.
  5. Deployment — single-process design deploys to any small VPS; a public read-only instance with a premium alert tier is the natural freemium shape if this ever becomes more than a side project.
